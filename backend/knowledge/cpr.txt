CPR (Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation) Procedure – Adult

1. Check responsiveness: tap the person and shout, “Are you okay?”
2. Call emergency services or ask someone nearby to call.
3. Check breathing for no more than 10 seconds.
4. If not breathing normally, begin chest compressions:
   - Place your hands in the center of the chest.
   - Push hard and fast, at a rate of 100–120 compressions per minute.
   - Depth: about 5–6 cm (2–2.5 inches).
5. Give rescue breaths if trained:
   - 30 compressions followed by 2 breaths.
6. Continue CPR until:
   - Professional help arrives,
   - An AED is available and ready to use,
   - The person starts breathing normally,
   - You are physically unable to continue.
